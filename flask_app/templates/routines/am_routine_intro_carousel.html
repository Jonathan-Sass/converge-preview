Initial morning practices

The following practices are the beginning of a morning routine.

Although they may seem inconsequential, they have benefits, and their simplicity is by design.

We begin with low barrier to entry practices that will help you build your own confidence in your ability to be disciplined and adopt new patterns.

Barriers and Strategies

At the core of Converge is beginning to understand your personal barriers, and addressing those head-on with strategies and 'habit anchors' to keep you moving forward.

We start everyone off with drinking a glass of water in the morning. This is because this is a universal need for humans, and for those of us who sometimes struggle to implement new patterns, it is a great place to start.

The key to success here is giving yourself an environmental cue that you can't miss. You can do this by placing a glass of water on your bedside table, or somewhere else you won't be able to miss it in the morning.

We are also going to use a technique called habit stacking, where we couple new habits with existing ones, or in this case with one that is exceedingly simple to serve as the cue for the next.

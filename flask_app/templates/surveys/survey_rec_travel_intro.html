THIS PAGE IS A PLACEHOLDER, KEEP GOING

Ever wonder why some people seem to crush their goals while others struggle to even get started? Spoiler: it’s all about their WHY. When you know what drives you, everything you do has a deeper meaning.

Understanding the neuroscience behind our motivations can profoundly impact how we approach personal growth and goal setting. At the core of our brain lies the limbic system, often referred to as the "lizard brain" or primal brain. This ancient part of your brain controls emotions and instincts, guiding your behavior from a deep, almost unconscious level. When we connect our actions to a deeper purpose or WHY, we engage the limbic system, tapping into these primal motivators that drive behavior at a fundamental level.

Numerous studies across health and wellness fields show that people with a strong sense of purpose are more likely to stick with their plans and achieve better results. For example, patients in rehabilitation who link their recovery to personal goals, like caring for family or achieving long-term dreams, tend to recover faster. In fitness, those who work out with a clear WHY—such as improving health for their children or training for a specific goal—are more consistent and stay motivated longer.

These examples highlight how having a deeper reason behind your actions makes you more persistent and successful in reaching your goals.
